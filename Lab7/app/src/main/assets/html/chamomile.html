Chamomile ( Latin:  Matricaria ) is a genus of annual flowering plants of the Asteraceae family , or Compositae ( Asteraceae ), according to modern classification, unites about 70 species [ 3 ] of low fragrant herbs [ 4 ] that bloom from the first year of life. The most famous species is Chamomile ( Matricaria chamomilla , syn. Matricaria recutita ), this plant is widely used for medicinal and cosmetic purposes.
